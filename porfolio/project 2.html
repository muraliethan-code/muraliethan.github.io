Global Inequality: Progress and Challenges in Reducing Disparities

Economic inequality continues to be a significant global issue, affecting millions of people across different regions.
While progress has been made in narrowing income gaps, challenges remain in ensuring equitable growth and eliminating social barriers.
Incomes of the poorest 40% of the population have generally increased at a faster rate than the national average in many countries, aided by financial transfers during the pandemic.
However, disparities between the richest and poorest nations have widened over the past five years. Additionally, persistent discrimination based on factors such as age, gender, religion, race,
and belief affects one in six people globally, reinforcing social inequality.

Regional disparities in income growth further illustrate the uneven progress in reducing economic inequality.
Data indicates that the bottom 40% of earners in 78% of Northern American and European countries have experienced faster income growth than the national average.
Conversely, only 30% of countries in Central and Southern Asia have achieved similar progress.
Despite financial transfers providing relief during the pandemic, long-term solutions require strategic policies to sustain inclusive prosperity and reduce income disparities across regions.
